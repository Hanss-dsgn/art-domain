"DIGITAL ARTS"

Everydays: The First 5000 Days

"Digital art by Beeple"

most famous digital work of the modern era, this is a massive collage of 5,000 individual images created daily by the artist over 13 years. It made history when it sold at Christie’s for $69.3 million, effectively launching the NFT craze into the mainstream.

The Flower of High Culture

"Digital art by Takashi Murakami"

Murakami is a traditional titan, his digital "Superflat" works bridge the gap between fine art and digital graphics. This piece uses vibrant, digitally rendered smiling flowers to critique the "flatness" of Japanese consumer culture and post-war identity.

Nyan Cat

"Digital art by Chris Torres"

It may be a GIF, but in the context of digital art history, it is an icon of "Internet Folk Art." This pixel-art animation of a cat with a Pop-Tart body flying through space represents the viral, memetic power that digital art holds over global culture.

Quantum

"Digital art by Kevin McCoy"

Historically significant for being the first NFT ever minted (originally created as a digital animation in 2014). It features a pulsating, neon-colored octagon that changes shape, symbolizing the birth of digital scarcity and ownership.

Human One

"Digital art by Beeple"

A "kinetic video sculpture," this piece features a digital astronaut walking through a changing landscape. Unlike a static image, the artist can remotely update the background data forever, making it a "living" piece of art that evolves with the world.
